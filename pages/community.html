The Neuroscience of Rest: Why Your Brain Needs Downtime

From Dr. Krista T. Kenney • May Edition

Dear Friend,

In our culture of constant productivity, rest is often viewed as laziness or luxury. But neuroscience tells us a different story: strategic rest is essential for peak cognitive function, creativity, and emotional wellbeing.

This month, I want to share three evidence-based rest strategies that have transformed my own work and helped countless clients move from burnout to sustainable productivity:

1. The 90-Minute Focus Block

Research shows our brains naturally cycle through periods of higher and lower alertness approximately every 90 minutes (called ultradian rhythms). Working with these cycles—rather than fighting against them—can dramatically improve both productivity and wellbeing.

Try this: Work in focused 90-minute blocks followed by 15-20 minute complete breaks. During breaks, step away from screens and do something physically and mentally different from your work.

2. Strategic "Unfocus" Time

When we deliberately unfocus our attention, we activate the brain's default mode network—the neural system responsible for insight, creativity, and connecting disparate ideas.

Try this: Schedule 10 minutes of "unfocus time" between tasks. Take a short walk, gaze out the window, or simply sit quietly without engaging with content. Notice what insights emerge when you give your brain this space.

3. Sleep as Cognitive Enhancement

Quality sleep isn't just about feeling rested—it's when your brain consolidates learning, processes emotions, and clears metabolic waste. Even slight sleep deprivation impairs cognitive function comparable to legal intoxication.

Try this: Create a 20-minute wind-down routine before bed that signals to your brain that it's time to transition to rest. Keep this routine consistent, even when traveling or during busy seasons.

Remember, rest isn't the absence of productivity—it's an essential component of sustainable high performance and wellbeing. Your brain was designed to oscillate between focused work and restorative rest.

What rest strategy will you implement this week? Reply to this email and let me know—I'd love to hear from you.

With grace and neuroscience,

Dr. Krista
